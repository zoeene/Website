From a young age I was constantly creating things. Once, on a hot afternoon with nothing to do, I put together a working cardboard looklike guitar, with serrated strips for the curved sides and stapled on rubber bands with varying colors for different notes. I also illustrated series of authored “books” - Mrs. Turtle and the Great Train Escape - made out of quartered A4 Printer Paper and purple staples.

I was and still am the textbook visual learner. A straight-A’s student who doodled in class while the teacher was talking. To study for upcoming tests and exams in the 8th grade at boarding school back home, I made custom textbooks: large ring bound notebooks filled with illustrated notes. From pictorial mnemonics to help me remember the order of the Periodic table, to storyboards showing the process of white blood cells on the offensive. I had a lot of these books, one for each subject and some for major exams (Checkpoint and the IGCSE). Plus they were a hit amongst friends and study groups.

I was most comfortable when I could picture things. My love for art later evolved into a passion for design. Design had always been a passive synonym till I understood its multifaceted nature, impact, and influence.

Design is art in action; discovering the existence and importance of design and the role it played and still plays in the decisions that have shaped progress - big or small, on the world stage or in my backyard - reassured me of the true importance artistic creativity has in everyday life.

We all "design" in many ways than none - regardless of whether we have the education, terminology or access to materials/technology and whether we consider our selves "creatively inclined". That's what I love most design; the fact that we are all inherent problem-solvers and use it in all we do. For example, in Nigeria, car-sharing has always been a societal response to poor and slow public transport; many people in that society rely on it to get around. It's been around long before Uber and the only thing that separates the two is the solid interface and system to connect the service to the user in a much easier way. Nonetheless, it is as qualified a design solution, just like my textbooks in boarding school were my solution to gaining a deeper understanding of what I was taught. They helped me move past cramming and onto true comprehension.

I am a firm believer in the power of "designing with" rather than "designing for" and advocate for design thinking as a tool of engagement and empowerment.
