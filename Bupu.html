Bupu - meaning “to export” in my native Igbo language - is a fictitious fair trade organization that promotes the support of communities of farmers and food manufacturers in regions of Nigeria, Kenya, and Botswana and also serves as a tasty platform for global cultural exchange.

It all started with the ENPOWER Free Trade Zone currenrtly being set up and commisioned in my home state of Enugu in Nigeria, by my father’s company and the state government. ENPOWER’s mission is to increase homegrown innovation, entrepreneurship and manufacturing; Bupu is my interpretation of the kind of organization that could theoretically work within and benefit from the Zone.

Through a subscription service, Bupu provides customers with preserved treats and snacks native to one of the three, or all, participating regions. These treats are made with the produce grown and harvested by the communities of farmers delivered every month with an accompanying recipe, ingredients and fun fact.

After conducting a quick survey, I found out that most people had tried or were familiar with North African and East African cuisine more than others. In order to address that, Bupu would thoeretically offer a wide variety of cuisine from new parts of the continent to push the brand's mission for creating an experience of cultural exchange, discovering new tastes and meeting the incredible people from different communities that make it all work.

I also researched other Fair trade organizations and food supscription services like Equal Exchange, Blue Apron and Fair Trade USA, to help me better understand Bupu's structure and visual identity principles within a similar context.

Logo Iteration

I wanted the Bupu logo to be vibrant and full of life and character, just like the food and people it represents. After going through different variations, I decided to go for a hand drawn brushscript. Since Bupu is, at its core, all about supporting real people, real handwriting was an honest depiction of the brand. I physically played around with different ink weights and script styles before getting a desired look.

Final Content

In the end I rendered the Bupu brand in various visual contexts. I designed potential packaging, a promotional poster, stationary, and the website.

This project was a semester long effort that culminated in a brand identity process book and presentation.
