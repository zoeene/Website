We are habitually creating, collecting, and sharing information. Online storage sites, such as Evernote, Google Drive, etc. allow for the all three to occur seamlessly on the same platform.

In an effort to learn HTML, CSS, and JQuery and further engage user research processes, I developed Pouch as an exploration into designing another option for this habit.

Pouch is a conceptual SaaS (Software as a Service) that provides content storage to its users. This project has its foundation in user research. I conducted surveys and wrote user personas to better understand why people loved to store and share stuff online. The Pouch homepage was eventually coded and was sent out for usability testing.

UX Research

To begin my design process, I sent out a survey to a group of people varying in age, geography, profession etc. I discovered that people save and share different things for different reasons. Some may have a preference for collecting information for majorly personal reasons (moodboard images and recipe notes) and others for work (relatable weblinks shared between coworkers), for example.

I identified the core needs and expectations of my general user when using an SaaS and then created User Personas with that information. Some valued security over all when storing and sharing information online while others desired an effective search function which would allow them find a specific thing amongst their large cache of stored informtation.

Logo iteration

Pouch's visual identity was derived with simplicity in mind. After deciding on "Pouch" as the website's name, I wanted a logo that would visualize the service's prioritized values and also be very relatable.
